# Security Reflection

During this cybersecurity assessment, I reviewed my personal device and online accounts to identify potential security risks. I found that using strong passwords and enabling Multi-Factor Authentication significantly improves account security. I also learned that keeping software updated reduces vulnerabilities that attackers may exploit.

One risk I identified was not enabling MFA on every account. Another concern was relying on memory for passwords instead of using a password manager. These practices can increase the chances of unauthorized access if passwords are compromised.

To improve my security immediately, I plan to enable MFA on all important accounts, regularly update my operating system, and use unique passwords for every service. I will also be more cautious when opening emails and clicking links to avoid phishing attacks.

Cybersecurity is important because our personal, financial, and professional information is increasingly stored online. Cybercriminals continuously develop new techniques to steal data and compromise systems. Understanding cybersecurity helps individuals protect their information and reduce risks.

This task increased my awareness of cyber threats such as phishing, malware, ransomware, social engineering, and data breaches. It also taught me the importance of developing good security habits and maintaining a proactive approach toward online safety.
